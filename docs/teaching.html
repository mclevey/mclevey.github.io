Undergraduate Courses

Sociology / Criminology 3040


Quantitative Research Methods

This course introduces students to foundational concepts, principles, and practices in contemporary quantitative social science. Core topics include research computing, data collection and processing, descriptive and inferential statistics, measurement, visualization, and statistical modelling. Research ethics, transparency and reproducibility, and effective communication are emphasized throughout.

This course follows the Faculty of Humanities and Social Sciences' Quantitative Reasoning Course Guidelines (see www.mun.ca/hss/qr).

Sociology / Criminology 4420


Networks, Culture, and Social Control

This course introduces students to social network analysis in sociology and criminology, emphasizing how patterns of relationships shape behavior, meaning, and social outcomes. Topics include personal networks, belief polarization, organized crime, and institutional deviance.

Workshops

Computational Social Science Symposium
January 2026, Dalhousie University, Halifax NS, Canada


Introduction to Topic Modelling

This workshop introduces the core concepts, methods, and underlying logic of topic modelling for research. It is designed for researchers with little or no prior experience in quantitative text analysis who want to work with text data in their own projects. Participants will learn how to prepare and process text data, fit topic models, and assess and interpret the results. Examples are provided in Python, but the content is not specific to any particular programming language or software.
