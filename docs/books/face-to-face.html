The Face-to-Face Principle and the Internet

Harry Collins, Rob Evans, Martin Innes, Will Mason-Wilkes, Eric Kennedy, and John McLevey
Cardiff University Press, 2022

An exploration of science, trust, truth, and democracy in the age of the internet. This book examines how the shift from face-to-face interaction to online communication affects the formation of expertise, the spread of knowledge, and the functioning of democratic institutions.

Key Themes

Expertise and Trust

How face-to-face interaction shapes the development and recognition of specialist expertise

Science and Democracy

The relationship between scientific knowledge and democratic decision-making

Online Communication

What is gained and lost when interactions move from physical to virtual spaces
