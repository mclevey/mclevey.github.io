<blockquote>
<p>SOCI/CRIM 3040, Quantitative Research Methods, introduces students to foundational concepts, principles, and practices in contemporary quantitative social science. Core topics include research computing, data collection and processing, descriptive and inferential statistics, measurement, visualization, and statistical modelling. Research ethics, transparency and reproducibility, and effective communication are emphasized throughout.</p>
<p>This course follows the Faculty of Humanities and Social Sciences' <strong>Quantitative Reasoning Course Guidelines</strong> (see <a href="http://www.mun.ca/hss/qr" target="_blank" rel="noopener">www.mun.ca/hss/qr</a>).</p>
</blockquote>

<h1 id="required-materials">Required Materials</h1>

<h2 id="statistics-software-jamovi"><a href="https://www.jamovi.org">Jamovi</a> (Statistics Software)</h2>
<p><strong><a href="https://www.jamovi.org">Jamovi</a></strong> is free/open source statistics software that provides an
    accessible interface for statistical analysis. We'll use Jamovi throughout this course for all lab assignments and
    hands-on exercises. Here are three ways you can use Jamovi:</p>
<ol type="1">
    <li><strong>LABNET Computers at MUN</strong>: Jamovi is pre-installed on computers in MUN's LABNET facilities, for
        example in our class lab (C2003). These computers are available during lab sessions and open lab hours.</li>
    <li><strong>Your Personal Computer</strong>: Jamovi can be downloaded and installed on your own laptop or desktop
        computer. This allows you to work on assignments at home and bring your laptop to class if you prefer.</li>
    <li><strong>Jamovi Cloud</strong>: If you don't have a suitable computer or can't access the LABNET facilities, you
        can use <a href="https://www.jamovi.org/cloud.html">Jamovi Cloud</a> through your web browser. A free account is
        available with limitations on storage and compute resources, but it provides full access to Jamovi's statistical
        analysis capabilities and is generally sufficient for course requirements.</li>
</ol>
<h3 id="download-and-setup-instructions">Download and Setup Instructions</h3>
<p>If you'd like to install Jamovi on your personal computer, follow these steps below. The installation process is
    straightforward and typically takes 5-10 minutes. No special configuration is required.</p>
<ol type="1">
    <li>Visit <a href="https://www.jamovi.org/download.html">www.jamovi.org/download.html</a></li>
    <li>Click the download button for your operating system</li>
    <li>Once the download completes, open the installer file</li>
    <li>Follow the on-screen installation prompts</li>
    <li>Launch Jamovi to verify it opens correctly</li>
</ol>
<p>We'll cover Jamovi basics in Week 3 of the course and then you'll develop your skills over the course of the
    semester. No prior experience with statistics software is required.</p>

<h1 id="assessment">Assessment</h1>
<br>
<p>You'll complete ten short online quizzes (4% each), two lab assignments, and one final examination. These assessment
    components are designed to evaluate both ongoing engagement with course material and cumulative learning outcomes.
</p>
<h2 id="quizzes-40">Quizzes (40%)</h2>
<p>You'll complete <strong>ten quizzes</strong> administered through Brightspace, each worth 4% of your final grade.
    These quizzes consist of multiple-choice and true/false questions and are open-book, so you can consult course
    materials while completing them. Quizzes emphasize content from assigned readings as well as material covered in
    lectures and labs. This format encourages regular engagement with course content while developing your ability to
    apply concepts and locate relevant information efficiently.</p>
<h2 id="lab-assignments-20">Lab Assignments (20%)</h2>
<p>You'll complete <strong>two lab assignments</strong> designed to provide hands-on experience with statistical
    analysis using Jamovi. These assignments will be completed across multiple lab sessions, with time provided in class
    to work through exercises. <em>You'll submit lab assignments twice during the term as collections of completed
        exercises.</em> Each submission is worth 10% of your final grade. These assignments provide opportunities to
    apply theoretical concepts to real data and develop proficiency with statistical software.</p>
<h2 id="final-exam-40">Final Exam (40%)</h2>
<p>There will be an <strong>cumulative, in-person, final exam</strong>. It will assess your understanding of course
    material, including key concepts, theoretical frameworks, and analytical methods covered throughout the term. The
    final exam questions will be very similar to the quiz questions you answer throughout the term, but unlike the
    quizzes it is closed-book and invigilated.</p>

<h2 id="course-policy-on-generative-artificial-intelligence-genai">Course Policy on Generative Artificial Intelligence
    (GenAI)</h2>
<p>The use of generative artificial intelligence (GenAI) tools in this course is <strong>permitted with the following
        guidelines</strong>:</p>
<ul>
    <li><strong>Attribution Required:</strong> Clearly document any use of GenAI tools. Example: "I used ChatGPT to help
        debug this function. The original error was X, and the AI suggested Y modification, which I then tested and
        verified." I will provide templates for documenting and disclosing your use of GenAI tools in Week 2.</li>
    <li><strong>Understanding Required:</strong> Use AI tools to help you understand concepts and debug problems, not to
        generate complete solutions you don't understand. If AI provides code, you must be able to explain how it works
        and modify it if needed.</li>
    <li><strong>Learning Progression:</strong> In early modules focused on learning fundamentals, prioritize writing
        code yourself with minimal AI assistance. In later modules, AI tools can be more helpful for complex tasks once
        you've demonstrated basic competency.</li>
    <li><strong>Responsibility:</strong> AI-generated code may contain errors or be inefficient. You are responsible for
        testing, understanding, and validating any AI-generated content before submission.</li>
</ul>
<p>Note that violations of academic integrity could result in automatic failures of the assignment or the course,
    depending on severity. All cases will be reported.</p>
